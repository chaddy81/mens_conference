Overview

Our families and communities are in need of men willing and able to lead others closer to Jesus Christ. Understanding what it is to be a man requires a strong life of faith and a good relationship with Him first.

The 2016 Green Bay Men’s Conference meets men wherever they’re at in their life. Whether you are Catholic or not, practicing your faith, or have been away for a lifetime. We’ll do this by connecting everyday guys with great speakers who will lift them up, give them courage to be more than they are today and depend on God’s strength to do so.

Men ages 12 to a 102 are welcome to join us for a day of prayer, engaging talks and connecting with organizations that will help them be more. And this conference will be more than a one day opportunity, it will be a beginning to a new chapter in your life as a man who leads, prays and lives with new life unlike far beyond what you’ve experienced before.

Coach Mike McCarthy

Mike McCarthy has been the head coach of the Green Bay Packers since January 2006, and led this team to a Super Bowl title with a 31-25 win over the Pittsburgh Steelers in Super Bowl XLV.

Born Michael John McCarthy on November 10, 1963, in Pittsburgh, he grew up one of five children in the Irish-Catholic family of father Joe and mother Ellen in Greenfield, a Pittsburgh neighborhood just a couple of miles from downtown. He earned a degree in business administration and was an all-conference tight end and senior captain in 1986 at Baker University in Baldwin City, Kansas.Then, he earned a master’s degree in sports administration while coaching linebackers at Fort Hays State under head coach John Vincent.

McCarthy’s family includes wife Jessica and their five children.

Since returning to Green Bay in 2006, Coach McCarthy has immersed himself in the local community and beyond through charitable donations and participation in numerous philanthropic events. Perhaps closest to his heart is the Mike & Jessica McCarthy Golf Invitational. Established in June 2010, the tournament benefits the American Family Children’s Hospital in Madison, Wisconsin. In addition to this, the McCarthy Family Foundation has sustained numerous charities, projects and institutions during his nine-plus seasons as head coach.

Jim VandeHey

Jim VandeHey is a husband, father of four children and co-founder of Catholic Leadership Retreats. CLR Ministries provides youth and young adults with an adventure experience of the Rocky Mountains where they encounter God, and develop a deeper personal relationship with Jesus Christ in the Eucharist.

Jim’s passion for family, ministry and the outdoors comes from his deep life of faith and experiences of being a lifelong Catholic.

He is a sought-after speaker for men and young adults, sharing his down-to-earth insights into spiritual growth and following the lead of the Holy Spirit. In addition to speaking wherever he’s asked, Jim also leads his own Hunting for God retreats and something wonderful called Man Camp.

Professionally, even though Jim and his family reside in Kaukauna, Wisconsin, he is the Land & Wildlife Manager at Smoky Hill Ranch in Gove, Kansas. And whether he’s at home or at the ranch, he enjoys time bow and rifle hunting with his family, playing disc golf and coaching youth sports.

Fr. Peter Mitchell

Fr. Peter Mitchell is the pastor of St. Mary of the Immaculate Conception in Greenville, Wisconsin. He was ordained to the priesthood in 1999 and holds a doctorate in Church History from the Pontifical Gregorian University in Rome. His priestly vocation was inspired by his own parents' faith and by the spiritual fatherhood of Saint John Paul II. His love for youth, families, and prayer forms the heart of his priestly ministry.

Fr. Peter is a dynamic speaker whose love for Saint Faustina and the Divine Mercy message has brought hope and healing to many people searching for God's love in the midst of our busy world.

In his free time, Fr. Peter personally is an avid distance runner, as well as enjoying time as a cross country and track coach at schools in his area.

Antonio J. Soave

Antonio J. Soave is a longtime, successful CEO in the business world who has been recently appointed as the Acting Secretary of Commerce for the State of Kansas. His experience extends to a variety of business sectors, most recently where he served as the Chairman, President and CEO of Capistrano-CGA.

Beyond his professional achievements, Antonio is the founder and current Chairman of the Global Foundation of Peace through Soccer, an international non-profit organization that spreads a message of “peace through soccer,” an International Advisory Council member of the United States Institute of Peace (USIP) and involved in promoting opportunities where the arts and sports meet.

Secretary Soave has a B.A. in International Studies from The American University in Washington, D.C., a Juris Doctor from the Detroit College of Law at Michigan State University, and a LLM (Masters of Law) in International Law from the University of San Diego. He is a former high school All-American athlete in the sport of soccer and he plays ice hockey as a hobby.

Harry Sydney

Harry Sydney played professional football for 10 seasons, and coached running backs with the Packers for six. He won Super Bowl Championships with the San Francisco 49ers in 1988 and 1989, and with the Green Bay Packers as their running backs coach in 1996. When Sydney retired from the NFL he launched a non-profit organization called "My Brother’s Keeper" because he wanted to make a difference in the community of Green Bay which he now called home.

He possesses a degree in criminology and juvenile justice from the University of Kansas. He and his wife have a blended family of eight children and 5 grandchildren.

Although Harry enjoyed a very successful career in the NFL, his path was full of disappointment and hard work. He came from a childhood filled with abuse, alcoholism, divorce and an environment filled with prejudice. Having overcome these obstacles, he has dedicated himself through the ministry he founded to help others find success in dealing with the hardships in their lives.

Harry’s willingness to work with men – one step at a time – brings forth positive change in their lives. His dedication to the boys and men in the program goes above and beyond the long hours at the office. He makes himself available at all hours to anyone who is in need, and is making a real difference in his community.
